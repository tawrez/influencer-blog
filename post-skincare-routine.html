The 10-Step Korean Skincare Routine Explained — What Each Step Actually Does

By InfluenceHub Team • Feb 25, 2026 • 8 min read

Influencer Priya Sharma recently posted a reel that hit 2 million views in 48 hours. In it, she walks through her 10-step Korean skincare routine. But a lot of people in the comments were confused — what does each step actually do? Do you need all 10? We watched the video 5 times and broke it all down for you.

“My skin was dull and patchy for years. Once I started this routine consistently, I saw a visible difference in just 3 weeks.” — Priya Sharma

Step 1: Oil Cleanser

The first step is an oil-based cleanser. Oil attracts oil, which means it pulls out sebum, SPF, and makeup from deep inside your pores. Priya uses a balm-to-oil cleanser that melts on contact with skin. This is especially important if you wear sunscreen daily — which you should.

What to look for: A cleanser with jojoba oil or mineral oil base. Avoid harsh sulphates here.

Step 2: Water-Based Cleanser

The second cleanse uses a gentle water-based cleanser to remove any remaining residue. This double-cleansing method is the foundation of Korean skincare. Priya uses a low-pH gel cleanser that does not disrupt the skin barrier.

Key ingredient: Look for ceramides or hyaluronic acid in your water-based cleanser to avoid stripping the skin.

Step 3: Exfoliant

Priya only exfoliates 2–3 times a week, not daily. She uses a chemical exfoliant with AHA (alpha hydroxy acid) which dissolves dead skin cells and reveals brighter skin underneath. Do NOT use a physical scrub — it creates micro-tears.

Step 4: Toner

Korean toners are nothing like Western astringent toners. They are lightweight, hydrating, and prep the skin to absorb everything that comes after. Priya pats her toner in with her hands rather than a cotton pad to avoid product waste.

Step 5: Essence

This is the heart of the Korean routine. An essence is a thin, water-like liquid packed with active ingredients — usually fermented extracts like galactomyces or snail secretion filtrate. It sounds unusual but the science backs it up for hydration and brightening.

Essences typically have the highest concentration of anti-ageing ingredients in the entire routine despite their watery texture.

Step 6: Serum / Ampoule

This is where you target specific concerns. Priya uses a Vitamin C serum in the morning for brightening and a retinol serum at night for anti-ageing. You should only use one active at a time, especially when starting out.

Step 7: Sheet Mask (2–3x per week)

Priya does a sheet mask on rest days. These are soaked in serum and seal in hydration. Leave on for 15–20 minutes, then pat the remaining essence into your skin — do not rinse.

Step 8: Eye Cream

The under-eye area has thinner skin and needs targeted care. Priya uses a peptide eye cream with a metal applicator to reduce puffiness. Apply with your ring finger using gentle tapping motions — never drag the skin.

Step 9: Moisturiser

Lock everything in with a moisturiser. Priya uses a gel-cream hybrid that feels lightweight but provides 24-hour hydration. Look for ingredients like hyaluronic acid, ceramides, and niacinamide.

Step 10: SPF (Morning Only)

The most important step in any routine, according to dermatologists. Priya uses SPF 50 PA++++ every single morning, even on cloudy days. This is non-negotiable if you are using any actives like Vitamin C or retinol.

Final Verdict

You do not need to do all 10 steps every single day. Start with the basics: double cleanse, moisturiser, and SPF. Add steps gradually as your skin adjusts. The most important thing Priya emphasises is consistency — a basic routine done daily beats a 10-step routine done occasionally.
